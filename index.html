What is Zero hunger?

Zero Hunger is one of the 17 Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs) established by the United Nations in 2015. Officially known as SDG 2, the goal is to end hunger, achieve food security and improved nutrition, and promote sustainable agriculture by 2030. The objective of Zero Hunger is multi-faceted and encompasses various dimensions, from eradicating hunger and malnutrition to fostering sustainable food production systems.
